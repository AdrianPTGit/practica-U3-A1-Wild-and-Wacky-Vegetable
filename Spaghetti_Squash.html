Spaghetti Squash: The Veggie That Turns into “Pasta”

Spaghetti squash is a type of winter squash known for its fibrous flesh that, when cooked, separates into long strands resembling spaghetti. This makes it a healthy, low-carb alternative to traditional pasta.

  • Shape and size: Usually oval, pale yellow to golden when ripe, and measures about 8–12 inches (20–30 cm) long.

  • Texture: Firm and compact when raw; when cooked, its fibers separate easily into spaghetti-like strands.

  • Flavor: Mild and slightly sweet, making it perfect for pairing with sauces, vegetables, or proteins.

Spaghetti squash is low in calories and carbs but rich in essential nutrients:

  • Fiber: Supports digestion and provides a feeling of fullness.

  • Vitamins: Contains vitamin C, B vitamins, and beta-carotene (a precursor of vitamin A).

  • Minerals: Good source of manganese, potassium, and calcium.

  • Baked: Cut in half, brush with a little oil, and bake until the flesh can be shredded with a fork.

  • Steamed: Cook until tender

  • Microwave: A quick method for busy days.
